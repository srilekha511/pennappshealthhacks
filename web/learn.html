What is Parkinson’s Disease?

Parkinson’s Disease (PD) is a neurodegenerative, chronic disease that affects movement. Specifically, Parkinson’s targets dopamine-producing neurons in an area of the brain called the substantia nigra.

What are the Symptoms of Parkinson’s Disease?

Symptoms often develop gradually over many years. The most common symptoms include tremors, bradykinesia, limb rigidity, and balance problems.
